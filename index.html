MDT-Bench: A Benchmark for Sequential Clinical Decision-Making in Multidisciplinary Teams

Abstract

We introduce MDT-Bench, a comprehensive multimodal benchmark designed to evaluate clinical decision-making in a realistic multidisciplinary team (MDT) setting. Comprising 12,910 samples sourced from the New England Journal of Medicine (NEJM) medical records and spanning over twenty clinical specialties (e.g., Pulmonary/Critical Care, Hematology/Oncology, Infectious Diseases), MDT-Bench transforms authentic cases into structured, multi-role and multi-turn dialogues. Each dialogue captures the sequential flow of medical processes, from initial patient screening and history-taking to diagnostic reasoning and treatment management. Our dataset uniquely models the interactive and collaborative nature of real clinical practice, where diverse expertise is integrated through dynamic feedback, error detection, and iterative decision refinement. By challenging foundation models to maintain clinical coherence, personalize reasoning, and integrate multimodal data, MDT-Bench bridges the gap between artificial and expert human performance in healthcare. Ultimately, this benchmark provides a vital tool for advancing research on collaborative medical decision-making, fostering improved AI support in clinical environments, and guiding the next generation of multidisciplinary medical AI systems.
